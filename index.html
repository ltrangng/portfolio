Say Hi, Hola, Servus, Chào

I am a UX Designer with a unique position in my domain of the Planning and Architecture industry, where I've been working as a landscape architect for 10+ years.

By mere chance I attended some Google's conferences in 2017, fascinated by the Tech world's dynamic culture. In day time I remain my role in the Architecture world. In night time I crawl the Web to learn programming, data analytics, product development etc. I am forever thankful to the Internet for leveling the playing field.

During Covid time I saw my two worlds merged into just a hyper-interconnected one, run by softwares. I moved between building teams in both the built environment and its virtual infrastructure. My work now ranges from 5 hectare local land to a 5.5 inch screen.

The design principles are the same, only either longer time frame strategy or short design cycle. The building process is extraordinarily similar, except that coding doesn't depend on the weather condition. Whilst Architecture is a highly regulated discipline, the Tech crowd is all quirks, rainbows and unicorns. Unlike Urban Design, digital products are not tied by a physical place or can only accessed by regional users.

For me Design is solving problems in an elegant way, and never a solo act. GitHub is full of open-sourced solutions waiting for teams to create products on top of it. I am interested in complex problems, and look forward to joining teams to tackle them.
