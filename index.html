    • Say Hi, Hola, Servus, Chào

    I am a UX designer with a profound 10+ year background in the planning and architecture industry. Urban Design is also a form of Experience Design, only that the product is tied to a physical place, and mainly accessed by regional users.

    I grew up in Vietnam without the Internet. There was no access to computers. There was even scanty food or medicine. Later I couchsurfed in diffenrent countries, easily learned to empathize with people's life and their diverse cultures, listened to their stories. Nations' borders are blurry constructed lines. Only people are real. My design philosophy simply grows from human- to humanity-centric.

    The world I knew has been altered by softwares. As an architect, I found myself moving between building teams in both built environment and its virtual infrastructure. My work now ranges from 5 hectare local land to a 5.5 inch screen. People I used to talk to over a beer or a coffee, now over Teams, Zoom, Google Meets, Slack, Jitsi, Keet.io etc. Never had I traveled this far like in the digital realm. My ideas are not bound by site work anymore, but shuffled, animated, filled with rainbows and unicorns, as far as the code can go.

    What matters more is my code of ethics. As a UX Designer, I stand on the frontline of defending the users.
